Avenger's Archive

One fateful summer, I decided to indulge myself in the popular phenonemon that was the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Ever since, I have followed each subsequent installement closely, dragging my friends to the premieres, making theories for future installments, and buying lots of merch. As an avid fan of the franchise, I thought I would create an introduction to the MCU and share some of my reviews!

Iron Man (2008)

Rating:

An enthralling start to the franchise. RDJ is so charasmatic, so that even when he was being a huge pain, you can't help but root for him. This is not a story about Superman, he's not the super sweet boy next door saving cats from burning buildings. Get ready to be thrust into the world of notorious billionaire, playboy, philanthropist Tony Stark as he embarks on a journey of self-discovery, empathy, and betrayal.

Hulk (2008)

Rating:

The black sheep of the MCU. Many within the fandom prefer to not acknowledge the existence of the movie. While it's definitely not my favorite, it is not unwatchable! Could you imagine being completely normal one day and being able to transform to an fantasy-like monster-creature overnight? Poor Bruce just wants to sleep but the government won't let him, smh. I wasn't totally sold on the romantic subplot of Betty and Bruce (chemistry was in fact NOT really there), but the story itself is cohesive. Don't get attached to Edward Norton, though. This is his first AND last appearance as Bruce in the MCU.

Iron Man 2 (2010)

Rating:

RDJ returns and gives a stellar performance as Tony Stark, now being forced to confront some of the repercussions of revealing his identity as Iron Man to the world. To be honest, the villain is pretty forgettable, but it's still a fun watch, I have rewatched multiple times. This movie also marks the introdcution of Scarlett Johansson as Black Widow! The wig leaves lots to be desired (lol) but her performance was stellar.

Thor (2011)

Rating:

This movie has AWFUL reviews, but I really liked it! It a great introduction to Thor and Chris Hemsworth is so adorable as the himbo God of Thunder. The pacing of the story and the redemption arc is one that you're actively rootiing for. Honestly, I don't have too much I dislike. However, everyone seems to hate the romance between Thor and Jane, but I thought it was cute enough. But that kiss at the end was so...weird and aggresive. 0/10. But other than that, great movie!

Captain America: The First Avenger Poster (2011)

Rating:

Honestly, I did not appreciate this movie for the awesome relationship building it was when I first watched it. Pay close attention to Bucky (if you don't future movies will get confusing REALLY quick)! To me, this is the first movie where you can start to feel the world building of the MCU and the connection of the different characters. Also consider Cap's character here, the transformation over the subsequent movies is simply ART.

Avengers (2012)

Rating:

I was SAT. All the initial movies were leading up to this moment. The Earth's Mightiest Heroes coming together for the first time to save the world. It's action-packed, filled with fun banter and iconic scenes. My soul practically left my body the first time the theme played and the camera circled the orginal six heroes: Captain America, Iron Man, Thor, the Hulk, Black Widow, and Hawkeye. This was one of those movies I wish I saw in theatres or could rewatch for the first time.
